PhD Student in Computer Science

University of Bayreuth · Efficient LLM Inference · KV Cache Compression · LLM Serving Systems

I am a PhD student working on efficient serving of large language models. My research focuses on making long-context and agentic LLM workloads faster and more memory-efficient, with a particular interest in KV cache compression, prefix reuse, and practical inference systems.

I am interested in the intersection of machine learning systems, data systems, and performance engineering: how models are served, how memory is managed during inference, and how system-level choices affect latency, throughput, and task quality.

Research interests

LLM inference systems

Serving architectures for long-context and agentic workloads, with attention to TTFT, prefill/decode behavior, scheduling, and throughput.

KV cache optimization

Compression, quantization, pruning, eviction, merging, and cache reuse techniques for reducing memory pressure while preserving model quality.

Benchmarking and evaluation

Workload-aware evaluation across task quality, latency, throughput, and memory, especially for long-context benchmarks.

Federated and open models

Model openness, post-training, and federated learning trade-offs across privacy, scalability, heterogeneity, and autonomy.

Selected projects

KV cache compression benchmark

A system-level benchmark comparing KV cache compression methods such as quantization, pruning/eviction, and merging. The project studies quality-efficiency trade-offs across long-context workloads and reports metrics such as TTFT, prefill latency, throughput, and KV memory.

Agentic workload serving analysis

Analysis of traces and serving behavior for agentic LLM workloads, focusing on long input/output distributions, prefix reuse opportunities, scheduling effects, and latency bottlenecks.

Federated learning and openness taxonomy

A research thread studying how open-source, open-weight, gray-box, and black-box model access affects federated post-training of foundation models.
